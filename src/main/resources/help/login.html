Login

The Mica Minecraft Launcher uses official Microsoft authentication to verify your Minecraft account. This is the same sign-in flow used by the official Minecraft launcher.

How It Works

  1. Click Sign In on the login screen. A browser window opens to the Microsoft sign-in page.
  2. Enter your Microsoft account credentials and approve the sign-in.
  3. Once authenticated, the browser window closes and the launcher proceeds to the Main Screen.

Token Caching

After a successful login, your authentication token is cached locally using AES-256-GCM encryption with a machine-derived key. This means:

  • You will not need to sign in again for approximately 4 hours.
  • The cached token is encrypted and tied to your specific machine — it cannot be transferred or reused elsewhere.
  • After the cache expires, the launcher will prompt you to sign in again.

Troubleshooting Login Issues

Internet Required

An active internet connection is required for the initial login. The launcher cannot authenticate in fully offline mode.

If you are having trouble signing in:

  • Browser does not open — check that a default browser is configured on your system.
  • Sign-in times out — verify your internet connection and try again.
  • Account not recognized — make sure you are using the Microsoft account that owns Minecraft.
  • Repeated sign-in prompts — the cached token may have expired. This is normal after 4 hours of inactivity.

For persistent issues, see Troubleshooting.
